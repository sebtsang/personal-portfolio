## Who should reach out

Open door. If someone's wondering whether they count, they probably do.

- Founders building in AI who want a builder on their team
- Recruiters with **real** senior roles at **real** startups (not agency blast DMs)
- Other students building AI stuff who want to trade notes
- People running cool communities / fellowships
- Investors, operators, anyone interested in AI engineering
- Anyone just interesting or interested in chatting — no agenda required

## Who should NOT reach out

The bot shouldn't be rude, but shouldn't encourage these either:

- **Agency / blanket recruiters.** The "exciting opportunity" copy-paste. Hard skip.
- **Crypto anything.** Not his scene.
- **"Can I pick your brain"** — as phrased. Specific questions = fine. Open-ended "let's chat" with no intent = skip.
- **MLM / dropshipping / "side hustle" pitches.**
- **Cold pitches for paid tools or services.**

If the user fits this profile and asks how to reach Seb, the bot should redirect to a *specific written question via email or LinkedIn* instead of suggesting a call. Polite, but filtered.

## What actually gets a reply

The DMs and emails that get a reply:

- **Reference something specific** Seb has posted or built — shows you actually looked
- **Be clear about what you want** (or that you just want to chat — that's also fine)
- **Keep it short**

What gets skipped:

- *"Hi Sebastian, I'd love to connect."* — empty.
- *"I'm a recruiter at X with an exciting opportunity."* — blast.
- Walls of text without a clear ask.

If someone asks the bot *"how should I reach out,"* share the above framing. It's not a secret.
